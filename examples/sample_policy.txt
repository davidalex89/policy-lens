ACME CORPORATION — INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
================================================
Version 2.1 | Effective Date: January 1, 2026

1. ACCESS CONTROL
-----------------
1.1 All users must be assigned a unique identifier before being granted access
    to any information system.
1.2 Access to systems and data shall be granted based on the principle of least
    privilege. Users shall only receive the minimum permissions necessary to
    perform their job functions.
1.3 Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all remote access and
    for access to systems containing sensitive data.
1.4 User accounts that have been inactive for more than 90 days shall be
    automatically disabled.
1.5 All privileged (administrator) access shall require a separate, dedicated
    account and shall be logged and reviewed quarterly.

2. INCIDENT RESPONSE
--------------------
2.1 The organization shall maintain an incident response plan that is reviewed
    and tested annually.
2.2 All employees must report suspected security incidents to the Security
    Operations Center (SOC) within 1 hour of discovery.
2.3 A post-incident review shall be conducted within 5 business days of
    incident closure to identify lessons learned and remediation actions.

3. DATA PROTECTION
------------------
3.1 Data at rest classified as Confidential or higher must be encrypted using
    AES-256 or equivalent.
3.2 Data in transit must be protected using TLS 1.2 or higher.
3.3 Removable media containing sensitive data must be encrypted and tracked
    in a media inventory log.

4. AUDIT AND LOGGING
--------------------
4.1 All information systems shall generate audit logs that capture, at a
    minimum: user ID, timestamp, event type, and success/failure indication.
4.2 Audit logs shall be retained for a minimum of one year.
4.3 Logs shall be reviewed weekly for anomalous activity by the security team.

5. CHANGE MANAGEMENT
--------------------
5.1 All changes to production systems must follow a documented change
    management process that includes request, review, approval, testing,
    and rollback procedures.
5.2 Emergency changes are permitted but must be documented and reviewed
    within 48 hours.

6. PERSONNEL SECURITY
---------------------
6.1 Background checks shall be conducted on all employees and contractors
    prior to granting system access.
6.2 Upon termination, all system access shall be revoked within 24 hours
    and company-owned equipment returned.
6.3 Non-disclosure agreements are required for all personnel with access
    to sensitive information.
